In linear algebra, LU factorization or decomposition (of Lower-Upper English) is a form of factorization of a matrix as the product of a lower and a higher triangular matrix. Due to the instability of this method, some special cases must be taken into account, for example, if one or more elements of the main diagonal of the matrix to be factored is zero, it is necessary to pre-multiply the matrix by one or several elementary permutation matrices. There is a second method called factorization PA = LU or LU with pivot. This decomposition is used in numerical analysis to solve systems of equations (more efficiently) or find inverse matrices.
